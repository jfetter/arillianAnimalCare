  4. Let pajama out of our bedroom (leave drywall panel "open") and tell him to go into his crate
  5. Follow him into laundry room and lock him into his crate -- give a tug to the cage to confirm that it is securely locked
  6. Open the zombie gate abd back door to let the 3-pack back in -- leave the zombie gate and back door open while you get jams
  7. Tell Huey to go into his crate
  8. Lock Huey in his crate tug twice to confirm its secure
  9. While standing in front of Huey's crate, unlock 'Jams crate and tell him to go outside
  10. Follow pajama to the back door, get him closed out of the zombie gate and lock him out for a few minutes
  11. Put chicken and rice (and doggy dailies if AM feeding) from fridge into each dogs' bowl
  12. Let Pajama back in and tell him to go into his crate / Lock him into his crate
  13. Their typical feeding order is: 1 Bella (pink bowl), 2 Pajama (blue plastic bowl), 3 Xena (all metal bowl), 4 Huey (blue bottom metal bowl)
  14. After they have finished eating, put Huey, Xena and Bella outside --outside of the zombie gate and let them go potty (while Pajama waits in his crate)
  15. Let the 3-pack back in and leave the zombie gate and back door open
  16. Put Huey back into his crate, check the lock, then, while standing in front of Huey's crate let Jams out and tell him to "go outside"
  17. follow Jams out and lock him outside (while Huey is still locked in his crate)
  18. After Jams has had a few minutes and/or hopefully pooped, let him back in and say "go to the bedroom" follow him to our bedroom and lock him in

  20. Let Huey out
  21. Make sure all doors are locked, the dry wall panel closed and lights are all out before you leave

Measuring and adding nutrients

I'll leave 3 "mixing containers" (old nutrient bottles), a tablespoon a funnel and the required nutrients on the kitchen counter next to the fridge.
All of the plants recieve the same nutrient mix: 1 tablespoon of "calMag", 1 tablespoon of "bloom nutirent", and 1 tablespoon of "grow nutrient"... use the funnel to put the nutrients into the bottles --then fill the bottle up with water after the nutrients are added.
To add nutrients to the plants, lift up the lid of each plant bucket and pour in nutrient water enough to bring the level into the bucket up to just below the bottom of the "root basket".
NOTE - You may not need all three gallons each day
